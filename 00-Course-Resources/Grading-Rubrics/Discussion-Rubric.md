# Discussion Rubric
**10 points per discussion | 5 discussions total**

## Initial Post (5 points) - Due Thursday 11:59 PM

**Purpose:** Apply course concepts to real-world scenarios using ethical analysis

| Criteria | Full Credit (5 pts) | Partial Credit (3 pts) | No Credit (0 pts) |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Length & Structure** | 150-200 words with clear organization | Under 150 or over 250 words; lacks structure | Significantly off word count or unorganized |
| **Ethical Analysis** | Identifies ethical issue, applies course framework, considers stakeholders | Mentions ethics but lacks framework or depth | No ethical analysis present |
| **Specific Examples** | Uses concrete example from work/life with details | Example present but vague or generic | No example provided |
| **Course Concepts** | Correctly applies 2+ course concepts with explanation | Applies 1 concept or application unclear | No course concepts applied |

## Peer Replies (2.5 points each, 5 points total) - Due Friday 11:59 PM

**Purpose:** Deepen understanding through peer-to-peer learning

**Each reply must be 75+ words and do ONE of the following:**

| Response Type | Full Credit (2.5 pts) | Partial Credit (1.5 pts) | No Credit (0 pts) |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Build on Ideas** | Extends peer's thinking with new perspective or additional course concept | Acknowledges peer but adds minimal substance | Simply agrees ("I agree") |
| **Ask Questions** | Poses thoughtful question that deepens analysis or explores implications | Asks basic clarification question | Question unrelated to post |
| **Offer Alternative** | Respectfully presents different viewpoint with reasoning | States disagreement without explanation | Dismissive or disrespectful |

## Tips for Success
✅ Use specific examples from your work or personal experience  
✅ Apply course concepts explicitly (name them!)  
✅ Consider multiple perspectives in your analysis  
✅ Engage meaningfully with peers - build on their ideas  
❌ Don't just say "I agree"  
❌ Don't wait until the last minute
